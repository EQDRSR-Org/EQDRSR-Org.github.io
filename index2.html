HERP – Hemlock Ecosystem Restoration Project The second-step project involved rescuing roadside at-risk & bonsai hemlocks, transplanting some up to one-footers into containers, and thinning up to 10-foot hemlock trees along with clearing brush and other competitive species. This led to spring 2024’s next step: feeding, thinning, and adelgid control — remarkably successful. The leaf growth and tree health exceeded expectations. April 4th update. The rescued potted and local 1'-10' Hemlocks are producing fresh light green growth. The majority are Adelphid free. I found two infected, likely ones I missed treating. The plan now is to transplant them around the two acre property along the Mineral creek side. The short ones up to 2-3 feet I will dig. The taller ones will need "grubbing" I.e. pulling straight up.
